About JATIC

The Joint AI Test Infrastructure Capability (JATIC) is a program developed by the Department of Defense's Chief Digital and Artificial Intelligence Office (CDAO).

JATIC's primary mission is to create software tools that accelerate and enhance the testing and evaluation (T&E) of artificial intelligence (AI) models across the DoD. By providing deeper insights into AI model performance, JATIC supports the DoD's broader mission of accelerating AI adoption and ensuring the development of responsible AI systems.

Features of JATIC

  • Python Libraries for AI T&E: These standalone libraries focus on specific aspects of AI testing and evaluation, allowing for easy deployment and integration into existing machine learning operations (MLOps) pipelines.
  • AI T&E Platform: Known as RAVEN, this data science platform provides open-source MLOps capabilities in an easy-to-deploy package, particularly beneficial for teams without an established MLOps pipeline.
  • Software Framework for AI Workflows: The `maite` library offers common types and protocols for AI objects such as models, datasets, and metrics, facilitating the development of seamless workflows using multiple tools.

By addressing key gaps in AI testing—such as robustness, reproducibility, and interoperability—JATIC empowers testers to assess the reliability, effectiveness, and safety of AI-enabled systems within the DoD.
